Background:

Liverpool FC is an English soccer team that has been historically successful in many regards. They are one of the most decorated clubs around the world, having won 6 European Championships (Champions League), which puts them as the third most successful team in the competition. Domestically, they have won 19 league titles, one behind Manchester United, their bitter rivals. They are one of the most followed teams globally. With an estimated 500 million fans worldwide, it has a claim for one of the biggest fanbases in the soccer world. The fans are very passionate, with the English team being known for their relentless support in both good and bad times, recalling their renowned anthem, "You'll Never Walk Alone". Needless to say, Liverpool are one the best clubs in soccer history; however, it is important to state that most of the team's accolades were won almost 50 years ago, with their golden age being around the 70's and 80's. Since then, they have gone through many ups and downs, but mainly downs. They failed to win a domestic league in 30 years, one of their longest title droughts. In this time, they were also failing to classify to Europe's top competition, the Champions League.

Recent times:

So, how can Liverpool go back to where they belong? How can Liverpool go back to the top of the footballing world? Well, in 2015 a big change was made. Jurgen Klopp was appointed as manager, and as he stated when he signed, he wanted to rebuild the club. Klopp said: “You have to change from a doubter to a believer. We have to start together new.” From then, Klopp fulfilled his promise. They ended their league drought in 2020, winning their first league title since 1990. They also won the most cherished trophy in world football, the Champions League, in 2019. Jurgen Klopp set back the standards for Liverpool, but there is still a long way to go for them to be considered the feared giants that they once were. So, with the basis for a successful team already established, what can they do to go back to the very top?

Database:¶

FiveThirtyEight's "Global Club Soccer Rankings" database will be very helpful to try to improve Liverpool FC and find solutions to make them a better team.

How does the database work?¶

The database has 641 rows, which means there is information about 641 different teams. There are also 7 columns: ranking (rank), previous ranking (prev_rank), name of the team (name), league in which the team plays (league), an offense statistic (off), a defense statistic (def), and most importantly, a rating system originally devised by FiveThirtyEight's editor-in-chief Nate Silver in for rating international soccer teams. This rating is called Soccer Power Index (SPI). The SPI is a system that estimates a team's overall strength. "In our system, every team has an offensive rating that represents the number of goals it would be expected to score against an average team on a neutral field, and a defensive rating that represents the number of goals it would be expected to concede. These ratings, in turn, produce an overall SPI rating, which represents the percentage of available points — a win is worth 3 points, a tie worth 1 point, and a loss worth 0 points — the team would be expected to take if that match were played over and over again" (Boyce, 2020). To read more how these ratings are performed, you can visit the following link: https://fivethirtyeight.com/methodology/how-our-club-soccer-predictions-work/

To reliably evaluate the accuracy of a data-driven predictor, we must always split our data into a training set and a test set. That is what is being done here. We scatter the teams by offense and defense statistics, dividing by seeing the teams better than average in red, and the teams worse than average in blue.
